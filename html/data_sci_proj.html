Causal Effects of Soda Tax

In lieu of the rising sugar content in beverages, the US government imposed a soda tax; Customers would have to pay an additional amount for beverages with sugar content.

But the main question was if the taxation was actually effective in reducing the consumption of aforementioned beverages.
The data of the purchase of the beverages was collected before and after the tax was introduced, and for bordering regions, one which had soda taxation, and the other did not.
It was found that even though the consumption of sugary beverages reduced in the taxed city, it was just due to the fact that Customers were willing to travel over to purchase the cheaper alternatives.
Housing Price Prediction (Kaggle)

Built a model to predict the price of a house with the Gradient Boosted Trees algorithm by performing appropriate feature modeling and preprocessing involving log transforms and normalizing the data.

Pipelined the entire procedure for stability and visibility, using the sklearn.pipeline module.
Achieved a RMSLE score of 0.13, with a pipelined model, ranking in the top 70th percentile.
Determined the most important predictors of housing price: Locality, number of rooms and bathrooms, live area in square feet.
